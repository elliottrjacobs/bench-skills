# Intake Question Bank

Extended question bank organized by category. The onboarding process uses a subset of these — pull from this bank when you need to go deeper on a specific area.

---

## Business Fundamentals

- What does the company/product do? (Explain it to a stranger.)
- What problem do you solve? For whom?
- What's the origin story? Why does this company exist?
- What stage are you at? (Idea, pre-launch, launched, scaling, pivoting, rebranding?)
- What's your business model? (SaaS, marketplace, services, hardware, etc.)
- What's your revenue model? (Subscription, usage-based, one-time, freemium?)
- How many people are on the team? What roles?

## Target Audience

- Who is your ideal customer? (Job title, company size, industry, situation.)
- What is their life like *before* they find you?
- What are they struggling with? What's the "struggling moment" that triggers action?
- What does success look like *after* working with you?
- What do your best customers say about you? (Actual quotes if available.)
- How do customers currently find you?
- What's the average deal size / customer lifetime value?
- Who are your worst-fit customers? Who should you NOT serve?

## Competitive Landscape

- Who are your top 3-5 competitors? (Direct substitutes.)
- What indirect alternatives exist? (Spreadsheets, manual processes, hiring, doing nothing.)
- What makes you different from each? (Be honest, not aspirational.)
- Where do competitors have an advantage over you?
- Is there anyone doing what you do that you admire? What specifically resonates?
- How do competitors position themselves? What do they emphasize?

## Goals & Engagement Scope

- What are you trying to accomplish with this engagement?
- What does success look like in 90 days? In 12 months?
- What decisions are you trying to make? (Launch, reposition, raise, sell, grow.)
- What's the most important deliverable from this work?
- What have you already tried that didn't work?
- Is there a specific event or deadline driving this? (Launch date, board meeting, funding round.)

## Constraints

- What's the budget for this engagement?
- What's the timeline?
- What's the team situation? (Who's doing the work after strategy is set?)
- Any technical limitations? (Legacy systems, platform lock-in, regulatory requirements.)
- Any brand/political constraints? (Things we can't say, competitors we can't mention, stakeholder sensitivities.)

## Existing Assets & Data

- Any existing brand work? (Name, logo, colors, voice guidelines, prior strategy.)
- Any customer research, surveys, interviews, or testimonials?
- Any metrics to work with? (Revenue, users, NPS, churn, conversion rates, CAC, LTV.)
- Any content or marketing assets? (Website, blog, social, email sequences.)
- Any sales materials? (Pitch deck, one-pagers, case studies.)
- Any product documentation? (PRDs, roadmaps, design files.)

## Culture & Preferences

- If your brand were a person, how would you describe their personality?
- 3 words you want associated with your brand? 3 you do NOT want?
- Any brands outside your industry whose style/voice you admire?
- How do you prefer to communicate? (Formal, casual, technical, conversational?)
- Any strong opinions about design, copy, or marketing that we should know?
