# Readiness Scorecard Rubrics

Score each dimension 1-5. Be honest — the value is in identifying gaps, not inflating scores.

---

## GTM Scorecard (8 dimensions, /40)

### 1. Positioning Clarity

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Cannot articulate what they do or who it's for in plain language |
| 2 | Can describe the product but not the differentiation — sounds like competitors |
| 3 | Has a positioning statement but it hasn't been validated with customers |
| 4 | Clear positioning that resonates with target audience, but no messaging matrix by persona/stage |
| 5 | Positioning is validated, documented, and used consistently across all GTM materials |

### 2. ICP Definition

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | "Anyone who needs [broad category]" — no specificity |
| 2 | Has a demographic profile but no buying behavior understanding |
| 3 | Knows the target company type and title but not buying triggers or process |
| 4 | Has ICP with firmographic + behavioral criteria, but no buying committee mapping |
| 5 | Full ICP with buying committee, triggers, process, negative personas, and language bank |

### 3. Competitive Understanding

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Cannot name competitors or only knows direct competitors |
| 2 | Can list competitors but doesn't know how they sell or position |
| 3 | Knows competitor products and pricing but not their GTM motions |
| 4 | Understands competitor GTM, messaging, and weaknesses — but no battlecards |
| 5 | Full competitive dossier with battlecards, win/loss patterns, and regular updates |

### 4. Sales Process

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No defined sales process — ad hoc conversations |
| 2 | Some pattern exists but it's in the founder's head, not documented |
| 3 | Defined stages (lead → qualified → demo → close) but inconsistent execution |
| 4 | Documented process with qualification criteria, but no playbook or enablement |
| 5 | Repeatable process with playbook, qualification framework, and measurable conversion between stages |

### 5. Marketing Engine

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No marketing channels active — word of mouth only |
| 2 | 1-2 channels active but not measured or optimized |
| 3 | Multiple channels active with some measurement, but no attribution to pipeline |
| 4 | Channels measured with pipeline attribution, but no content strategy or calendar |
| 5 | Full marketing engine: content strategy, measured channels, pipeline attribution, and optimization cadence |

### 6. Enablement

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No sales materials beyond the product itself |
| 2 | Has a pitch deck but nothing else (no battlecards, scripts, or objection guides) |
| 3 | Pitch deck + some one-pagers or case studies, but no structured playbook |
| 4 | Playbook exists with objection handling and demo script, but no competitive battlecards |
| 5 | Full enablement suite: playbook, battlecards, objection guide, discovery questions, demo script, case studies |

### 7. Metrics

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Not tracking any GTM metrics |
| 2 | Tracking revenue/users but no funnel metrics (CAC, conversion rates, etc.) |
| 3 | Tracking funnel metrics but no benchmarks or regular review cadence |
| 4 | Funnel metrics with benchmarks and monthly review, but no leading indicators |
| 5 | Full metrics hierarchy (north star → leading → lagging → diagnostic) with dashboards and operating review cadence |

### 8. Team

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Founder doing everything alone — no GTM help |
| 2 | 1-2 people touching GTM but no clear roles or accountability |
| 3 | Defined GTM roles but gaps in coverage (e.g., no content, no SDR) |
| 4 | Core GTM team in place with clear roles, but missing key hires |
| 5 | Full GTM team with clear DRIs, reasonable capacity, and a hiring plan for gaps |

### GTM Score Interpretation

| Total (/40) | Assessment | Recommendation |
|-------------|-----------|----------------|
| 8-15 | **Early stage** — Foundational work needed | Start with /gtm-icp → /gtm-positioning |
| 16-24 | **Building** — Good foundation, gaps to fill | Prioritize the 2-3 lowest dimensions |
| 25-32 | **Maturing** — Solid GTM, optimization opportunities | Focus on enablement, metrics, and feedback loops |
| 33-40 | **Advanced** — Execution and scaling focus | Fine-tune with competitive intel updates and win/loss analysis |

### GTM Priority Chain

1. **ICP + Competitive Intel** before Positioning (know who you're positioning for and against)
2. **Positioning** before Strategy (positioning drives the GTM plan)
3. **Strategy** before Pricing, Outreach, Marketing (these execute the strategy)
4. **Meeting Prep** can run anytime — it's a daily workhorse

## Product Scorecard (6 dimensions, /30)

### 1. Problem Clarity

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Vague idea — can't articulate the problem or who has it |
| 2 | Has a hypothesis but hasn't talked to potential users |
| 3 | Has talked to users but findings are anecdotal, not structured |
| 4 | Structured research (interviews, JTBD) with clear problem definition |
| 5 | Validated problem with evidence — users actively seeking solutions |

### 2. User Research Depth

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No user research conducted |
| 2 | Informal conversations with friends/family |
| 3 | 5-10 structured user interviews |
| 4 | Ongoing research program with interview guides and synthesis |
| 5 | Continuous discovery — regular interviews, usability tests, and data analysis |

### 3. PMF Signals

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Pre-product — no users yet |
| 2 | Have users but no retention data |
| 3 | Some retention but inconsistent — unclear if PMF exists |
| 4 | Strong retention in a segment, organic word-of-mouth emerging |
| 5 | Clear PMF — high retention, organic growth, users would be "very disappointed" without it |

### 4. Product Analytics

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No analytics instrumented |
| 2 | Basic pageview/session tracking only |
| 3 | Event tracking on key actions but no regular review |
| 4 | Funnel analytics with conversion tracking and regular review |
| 5 | Full instrumentation with cohort analysis, retention curves, and feature adoption tracking |

### 5. Prioritization Rigor

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No prioritization — building whatever feels right |
| 2 | Founder intuition only |
| 3 | Customer requests drive roadmap (reactive) |
| 4 | Structured framework (RICE, opportunity scoring) with some data |
| 5 | Data-informed prioritization tied to outcomes, regularly reviewed |

### 6. Technical Foundation

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No code yet — concept stage |
| 2 | Prototype / proof of concept |
| 3 | MVP with significant technical debt |
| 4 | Solid codebase with some architectural concerns |
| 5 | Well-architected, tested, deployable, and scalable |

## Design/Brand Scorecard (5 dimensions, /25)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|

| 2 | Has a name and basic visual but no brand strategy |
| 3 | Some brand thinking but not documented or consistent |
| 4 | Documented brand strategy (positioning, values, personality) |

| 2 | Ad hoc components, no consistency |
| 3 | Partial component library covering basics |

### 4. Voice & Tone Definition

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | No voice or tone defined |
| 2 | Informal sense of voice but nothing written down |
| 3 | Basic voice guidelines (3-5 adjectives) |
| 4 | Voice guidelines with tone variations by context (marketing, support, social) |
| 5 | Full voice framework with do/don't examples, sample copy, and tone spectrum |

### 5. Brand Consistency

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 1 | Every touchpoint looks and sounds different |
| 2 | Some consistency in visuals but voice varies widely |
| 3 | Consistent in primary channels, inconsistent in secondary |
| 4 | Consistent across most touchpoints with minor gaps |
| 5 | Cohesive experience across all touchpoints — brand is unmistakable |

## Engineering Scorecard (5 dimensions, /25)

| 4 | Defined workflow with branching strategy, code review, and release process |

## Intake Question Bank

Extended question bank organized by category. Pull from this bank when you need to go deeper on a specific area.

### Business Fundamentals
- What does the company/product do? (Explain it to a stranger.)
- What's the origin story? Why does this company exist?
- What's your business model? (SaaS, marketplace, services, hardware, etc.)
- How many people are on the team? What roles?

### Target Audience (Extended)
- What do your best customers say about you? (Actual quotes if available.)
- How do customers currently find you?
- What's the average deal size / customer lifetime value?
- Who are your worst-fit customers? Who should you NOT serve?

### Competitive Landscape (Extended)
- Where do competitors have an advantage over you?
- Is there anyone doing what you do that you admire? What specifically resonates?
- How do competitors position themselves? What do they emphasize?

### Goals (Extended)
- What decisions are you trying to make? (Launch, reposition, raise, sell, grow.)
- What's the most important deliverable from this work?
- What have you already tried that didn't work?
- Is there a specific event or deadline driving this?

### Constraints (Extended)
- Any brand/political constraints? (Things we can't say, competitors we can't mention, stakeholder sensitivities.)

### Existing Assets
- Any content or marketing assets? (Website, blog, social, email sequences.)
- Any sales materials? (Pitch deck, one-pagers, case studies.)
- Any product documentation? (PRDs, roadmaps, design files.)
- Any customer research, surveys, interviews, or testimonials?
- Any metrics to work with? (Revenue, users, NPS, churn, conversion rates, CAC, LTV.)
