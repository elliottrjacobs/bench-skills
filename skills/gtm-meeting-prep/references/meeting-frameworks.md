# Meeting Preparation Frameworks

---

## Discovery Call Structure (30 min)

| Time | Phase | What to Do |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 0-2 min | **Rapport** | Reference something personal (their content, mutual connection, recent news). Keep warm but brief. |
| 2-5 min | **Set agenda** | "I'd love to learn about [their situation]. Then I'll share a bit about how we might help. Does that work?" Get permission to ask questions. |
| 5-20 min | **Discovery** | SPIN questions adapted to their context. Listen 70%, talk 30%. Take notes on their exact words. |
| 20-25 min | **Initial value** | Connect what they shared to how you help. Use their language, not yours. "You mentioned [pain]. Here's how we address that..." |
| 25-30 min | **Next steps** | Specific: "Based on what you shared, I think a demo focused on [their use case] makes sense. How does [specific date] work?" |

### Discovery Call Rules
- Never pitch in a discovery call. Your job is to understand, not convince.
- Write down their exact phrases — you'll use them in follow-up and proposals
- Ask "Why is this a priority now?" — the compelling event determines deal urgency
- End with a clear next step — vague outcomes kill momentum

## Demo Structure (45 min)

| Time | Phase | What to Do |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 0-3 min | **Intro & agenda** | Brief intro, confirm agenda, ask if priorities have changed since last conversation |
| 3-8 min | **Re-confirm pain** | "Last time you mentioned [pain]. Is that still the top priority? Anything change?" |
| 8-12 min | **Promised land** | Show the vision — what their world looks like with your solution. Outcomes, not features. |
| 12-35 min | **Live demo** | Walk through THEIR use case. Stop every 5 min: "How does this compare to what you do today?" |
| 35-40 min | **Proof** | Case study from a similar company. Specific metrics. "They were in your exact situation." |
| 40-45 min | **Next steps** | Propose mutual action plan. "Based on today, here's what I'd recommend as next steps..." |

### Demo Rules
- Customize to their use case — never give a generic tour
- Show the value before the feature: "This means you can [outcome]" before clicking
- If they ask about a feature you don't have: "Great question. We handle that differently — [explain approach]"
- Track "aha moments" — when their eyes light up, that's your positioning talking point

## Investor Meeting Structure (30-45 min)

| Time | Phase | What to Do |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 0-2 min | **Context** | How you got here, why you're excited about this meeting |
| 2-7 min | **The shift** | Market shift happening now (Raskin framework). Why now? |
| 7-12 min | **What we do** | Product, who it's for, how it works (keep simple) |
| 12-18 min | **Traction** | Metrics, customers, growth, retention. Be specific. |
| 18-25 min | **Why we win** | Differentiation, moat, competitive advantages |
| 25-30 min | **The ask** | Round size, use of funds, timeline |
| 30-45 min | **Q&A** | Be direct. "I don't know" is better than a vague answer. |

---

## Partnership Meeting Structure (30 min)

| Time | Phase | What to Do |
|------|-------|-----------|
| 0-3 min | **Context** | Why this partnership makes sense for both |
| 3-10 min | **Mutual value** | What each side gets. Be specific about the value exchange. |
| 10-20 min | **How it works** | Integration points, go-to-market together, customer experience |
| 20-25 min | **Concerns** | Address risks openly. "What would need to be true for this to work?" |
| 25-30 min | **Next steps** | Specific actions for both sides with timelines |

## Pre-Meeting Research Checklist

### Company Intel
- [ ] What they do (product, market, business model)
- [ ] Size (employees, revenue if public, funding stage)
- [ ] Recent news (last 6 months — launches, funding, leadership changes)
- [ ] Current initiatives (from blog, job postings, press)
- [ ] Tech stack (from job postings, BuiltWith)
- [ ] Competitors they face

### Person Intel (per attendee)
- [ ] Current title and tenure
- [ ] Previous companies and roles
- [ ] LinkedIn activity (recent posts, interests)
- [ ] Mutual connections
- [ ] What they likely care about based on their role

### Competitive Intel
- [ ] Are they evaluating competitors? Which ones?
- [ ] Pull relevant battlecard
- [ ] Prepare differentiation talking points

### Preparation
- [ ] 5-7 tailored discovery questions
- [ ] 3-5 likely objections with responses
- [ ] Relevant case study or proof point
- [ ] Follow-up email template drafted
- [ ] Calendar hold for follow-up (tentative)

## Follow-Up Best Practices

### Timing
- Send follow-up within 2 hours of the meeting (same day minimum)
- Include meeting notes and agreed-upon next steps
- Attach any promised resources

### Format
```
Subject: [Meeting recap] [Specific next step]

[Name],

Thanks for the time today. Key takeaways:

1. [Their stated pain/priority — in their words]
2. [What we discussed as the approach]
3. [Agreed next step with specific date]

[Relevant attachment: case study, resource, or one-pager]

I'll [your action item] by [date]. Let me know if anything changes on your end.

[Your name]
```

### Follow-Up Rules
- Reference THEIR words, not yours
- Be specific about next steps and timelines
- Include one relevant resource (not five)
- Set a calendar reminder to follow up if they don't respond within 3 days
