## Battlecard Template

One page per competitor. Must be usable mid-call — keep it scannable.

### Structure

```markdown
# [Competitor Name] — Battlecard

**Last Updated:** YYYY-MM-DD
**Win Rate Against Them:** X% (if known)

## Their Pitch (in 2 sentences)
[How they position themselves — their elevator pitch]

## Their Strengths (be honest)
- [Strength 1 — acknowledge what they do well]
- [Strength 2]
- [Strength 3]

## Their Weaknesses (from reviews + deal intel)
- [Weakness 1 — specific, verifiable, from buyer perspective]
- [Weakness 2]
- [Weakness 3]

## How to Win Against Them
- [Your differentiator that matters most in this matchup]
- [Proof point: customer quote, data, case study]
- [The narrative: why our approach is fundamentally better for THIS buyer]

## Landmine Questions
Ask the prospect these questions to expose competitor weaknesses:
- "[Question that highlights a weakness they can't fix]"
- "[Question about a capability they lack]"
- "[Question about long-term implications of their approach]"

## Talk Track — When They Come Up
"[Competitor] is a solid option for [acknowledge their strength].
Where we differ is [your key differentiator].
For teams like yours that need [their specific requirement], that matters because [connect to their pain].
[Reference]: [Similar company] chose us over [competitor] because [specific reason]."

## When We Lose (be honest)
- [Scenario where they're genuinely better]
- [Types of buyers who prefer their approach]
- [Consider disqualifying if these conditions are true]
```

## Competitive Matrix Methodology

### Dimensions to Compare

**Product:**
- Core capabilities and feature set
- Integration ecosystem
- Technical architecture (cloud, on-prem, hybrid)
- API quality and extensibility

**Market Position:**
- Positioning statement / tagline
- Target market segment
- Market share or estimated size
- Brand perception

**GTM:**
- Primary GTM motion (PLG, SLG, community, hybrid)
- Sales team structure and size
- Marketing channels and content strategy
- Pricing model and price points
- Key partnerships or channel strategy

**Business:**
- Funding stage and total raised
- Revenue estimates (if available)
- Team size and growth trajectory
- Recent M&A or strategic moves

### Scoring

Rate each competitor 1-5 on each dimension relative to the market (not relative to you):
- 1 = Significant weakness
- 3 = Market parity
- 5 = Clear market leader in this dimension

## Competitive GTM Analysis Framework

For each competitor, map their GTM motion:

### Acquisition Channels
- **Organic:** SEO, content marketing, community, word of mouth
- **Paid:** Google Ads, social ads, sponsorships, events
- **Outbound:** SDR team, cold email, LinkedIn outreach
- **Partnerships:** Technology partners, agency partners, resellers
- **Product-led:** Free tier, freemium, self-serve trial

### Sales Process
- **Entry point:** How do prospects first engage?
- **Qualification:** How do they filter leads?
- **Demo/Trial:** What does the evaluation look like?
- **Closing:** Who's involved, what's the process?
- **Timeline:** Typical sales cycle length

### Content Strategy
- **Blog frequency and topics** — What do they publish and how often?
- **Gated content** — What do they gate (and what does that tell you about their funnel)?
- **Social presence** — Where are they active and what's their voice?
- **Thought leadership** — Who are their spokesperson(s)?

### Customer Success
- **Onboarding model** — Self-serve, guided, white-glove?
- **Support model** — Email, chat, phone, dedicated CSM?
- **Community** — Do they have a user community? How active?
- **Expansion motion** — How do they grow existing accounts?
