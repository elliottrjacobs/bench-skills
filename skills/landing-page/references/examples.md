# Award-Winning Landing Page Examples

A curated collection of high-performing landing pages organized by what they do well. Use these for inspiration, not imitation — study the principles, then apply them to your context.

---

## Product / SaaS

### Linear
**What they nail:** The hero IS the demo. Shows the product in action immediately instead of describing it. Narrative headline with supporting visual that tells the story in seconds before you scroll.
**Lesson:** If your product is visually impressive, let it speak. A screenshot-first hero can outperform a copy-first hero for solution-aware audiences.

### Notion
**What they nail:** Narrative headline + supporting visual. Communicates flexibility without overwhelming. Clean, spacious layout.
**Lesson:** When your product does many things, don't list them all. Pick the one narrative that resonates and commit.

### Paste App (pasteapp.io)
**What they nail:** Simplifies complex functionality with clean layout and crystal-clear value proposition. Built for power users but doesn't feel intimidating.
**Lesson:** Simplicity in presentation doesn't mean simplicity in product. Complex tools can have clean landing pages.

### Framer
**What they nail:** Visually shows product value above the fold. Interactive elements that demonstrate capabilities. Strong "how it works" section.
**Lesson:** Interactive hero elements can boost engagement, but only if they reinforce the value proposition (not just look cool).

## Trust & Social Proof Excellence

### Netflix
**What they nail:** Minimal copy, single CTA, social proof built into the brand itself. Extreme simplicity.
**Lesson:** When brand awareness is high, strip everything down to the CTA. Netflix doesn't need to explain what streaming is.

### Dropbox Brand (brand.dropbox.com)
**What they nail:** Interactive grid layout with animated modules. Makes brand guidelines (normally boring) feel explorable and functional.
**Lesson:** Interactivity can make even dry content engaging. The medium can reinforce the message.

### Superpower (superpower.com)
**What they nail:** Futuristic animated data storytelling for personalized health insights. Design quality itself communicates trustworthiness.
**Lesson:** In health/fintech/security, design quality IS trust. A polished page signals a reliable product.

## Narrative & Storytelling

### Pasqua Wines (pasqua.it)
**What they nail:** Immersive scroll-based storytelling. Balances heritage with modern interaction. Makes wine exploration feel like an experience.
**Lesson:** For brand-building landing pages, the journey IS the conversion. Not every page needs a hard CTA.

### Unveil (unveil.fr)
**What they nail:** Structures content as cinematic chapters. Portfolio as narrative experience, not a list.
**Lesson:** Sequential storytelling (chapter-based scrolling) works when the audience is browsing, not buying.

### Getty Gehry Archive (gehry.getty.edu)
**What they nail:** High-resolution imagery with intuitive filtering. Proof that information-dense pages can be elegant.
**Lesson:** Content-heavy pages succeed when filtering and navigation are exceptional. Information architecture matters.

---

## Bold & Experimental

### Obys AIM (aim.obys.agency)
**What they nail:** Kinetic typography and scroll-based storytelling. Pushes creative boundaries while maintaining navigational clarity.
**Lesson:** Experimental design works when navigation remains intuitive. Bold choices fail when visitors get lost.

### Cosmos (cosmos.so)
**What they nail:** Floating visual cards and dynamic exploration. Invites curiosity without overwhelming.
**Lesson:** Discovery-based interfaces work for creative tools. Let the visitor explore rather than follow a prescribed path.

### Silencio (silencio.es)
**What they nail:** Bold typography and motion effects blending portfolio with product shop.
**Lesson:** Typography can be the hero visual. When type is bold enough, you don't need illustrations.

---

## High-Converting Classics

### Basecamp
**What they nail:** Before/after framing. Shows the chaotic "before" vs. calm "after." Extremely specific testimonials with real numbers.
**Lesson:** Before/after is one of the most persuasive structures. If you can show transformation, use it.

### Shopify
**What they nail:** "Start free trial" CTA with just an email field. Minimal friction. Massive social proof ("millions of businesses").
**Lesson:** The fewer fields in your form, the higher your conversion. Shopify asks for one thing: an email.

## Patterns Across Winners

| Pattern | Why It Works |
|---------|-------------|
| Product shown in context in the hero | Reduces abstraction. Visitors see themselves using it. |
| Single email field for signup | Minimum viable friction. Get them in, qualify later. |
| Specific metrics in testimonials | Numbers create believability. Vague praise is invisible. |
| Bold typography as design element | Reduces dependency on imagery. Type can carry the page. |
| Scroll-based progressive reveal | Keeps visitors engaged by rewarding scrolling with new content. |
| CTA repeated at hero, mid-page, end | Catches visitors at different decision points. |
