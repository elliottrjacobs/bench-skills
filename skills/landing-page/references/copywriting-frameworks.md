# Landing Page Copywriting Frameworks

Proven frameworks for structuring landing page copy. Choose based on your audience's awareness level and the complexity of your offer.

---

## PAS — Problem, Agitate, Solution

**Best for:** Problem-aware audiences. Short to medium-length pages. Most versatile framework.

| Step | Job | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Problem** | Name the specific pain the visitor experiences | "Deploying takes your team 45 minutes every time — and something still breaks." |
| **Agitate** | Twist the knife. Show the cost of inaction. | "That's 15 hours a month wasted on deployments. And every failed deploy means another late night, another Slack fire drill, another engineer thinking about quitting." |
| **Solution** | Present your product as the fix | "Shipfast deploys in under 3 minutes with zero-downtime rollbacks. Your team ships more, stresses less." |

**When to use:** Default choice for most landing pages. Works well when the audience knows they have a problem but hasn't found a solution yet.

**Tip:** Spend more time on Agitate than you think. Most writers rush to the solution. The agitation is what creates the emotional urgency to act.

---

## PPPP — Picture, Promise, Prove, Push

**Best for:** Aspiration-driven products. Audiences motivated by outcomes rather than escaping pain.

| Step | Job | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Picture** | Paint a vivid image of the desired future state | "Imagine opening your laptop Monday morning to a dashboard where every deploy from the weekend went perfectly. No alerts. No rollbacks. Just shipped features." |
| **Promise** | Commit to delivering that outcome | "Shipfast makes zero-stress deployments the default, not the exception." |
| **Prove** | Back it up with evidence | "12,000 teams deploy with Shipfast daily. Average deploy time: 2 minutes 40 seconds. Rollback rate: 0.3%." |
| **Push** | Drive toward action | "Start your free trial — your first deploy is 5 minutes away." |

**When to use:** When selling a positive transformation rather than solving an acute pain. Works well for lifestyle products, premium tools, and aspirational brands.

**Tip:** The Picture step must be specific and sensory. "Imagine being more productive" is weak. "Imagine closing your laptop at 5pm because everything shipped on time" is strong.

---

## PRESTO — Pain, Resonate, Educate, Simplify, Testify, Offer

**Best for:** Higher-consideration purchases. Longer landing pages. Products that require education.

| Step | Job | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Pain** | Identify the core frustration | "Your deployment pipeline is a patchwork of scripts, hope, and 3am alerts." |
| **Resonate** | Show you deeply understand the consequences | "We've talked to 500+ engineering leads. The #1 thing they say: 'I'm losing good engineers because our deploy process is broken.' The cost isn't just time — it's talent." |
| **Educate** | Explain what a solution looks like (without pitching yet) | "The best teams have 3 things: automated rollbacks, deploy previews, and sub-5-minute pipelines. Here's why each matters..." |
| **Simplify** | Position your product as the easy path | "Shipfast gives you all three out of the box. One config file. One command. Done." |
| **Testify** | Let customers prove it | "After switching to Shipfast, deploy failures dropped 94% and we shipped 3x more features per sprint. — Sarah Chen, VP Eng at Acme" |
| **Offer** | Make it easy to start | "Free for teams under 10. No credit card. Deploy your first app in 5 minutes." |

**When to use:** When the audience needs to be educated before they can evaluate your solution. Enterprise products, technical tools, or anything with a learning curve.

**Tip:** The Educate step is what differentiates PRESTO. You're building authority by teaching — not selling — before you introduce your product.

---

## AIDA — Attention, Interest, Desire, Action

**Best for:** Simple offers, single-product pages, e-commerce. The classic direct response framework.

| Step | Job | Example |
|------|-----|---------|
| **Attention** | Stop the scroll with a bold headline | "Your toothbrush is lying to you." |
| **Interest** | Hook them with a surprising fact or insight | "Most electric toothbrushes clean 40% of your teeth. The other 60%? Missed entirely." |
| **Desire** | Make them want what you have | "Goby's sonic technology reaches every surface, every time. Dentist-clean feeling, every morning." |
| **Action** | Tell them exactly what to do | "Try Goby risk-free for 60 days. Free shipping, free returns." |

**When to use:** Quick-decision products. Low consideration, clear value, impulse-friendly.

---

## Choosing the Right Framework

| Audience Awareness | Recommended Framework |
|---|---|
| **Unaware** (don't know they have a problem) | PRESTO — educate first |
| **Problem-aware** (know the pain, seeking solutions) | PAS — agitate and solve |
| **Solution-aware** (comparing options) | PPPP — paint the better outcome |
| **Product-aware** (know you, need a push) | AIDA — grab attention and drive action |

---

## Universal Copy Rules

These apply regardless of framework:

1. **Headline carries everything.** 8/10 people read only the headline. Spend 50% of your writing time on it.
2. **Write about the customer, not yourself.** "You'll ship faster" > "We built a platform."
3. **Be specific.** Vague claims are invisible. Numbers, timeframes, and concrete outcomes create believability.
4. **Use their words.** Mine reviews, support tickets, and sales calls for the exact language your customers use.
5. **Every sentence earns the next.** If a sentence doesn't make the reader want to read the next one, cut it.
6. **One idea per section.** Don't overload any single block with multiple arguments.
7. **CTA uses outcome language.** "Start free trial" > "Submit." "Get the guide" > "Download." The button should name what the visitor gets.
8. **Kill adverbs and adjectives.** "Deploys in 3 minutes" is stronger than "Incredibly fast deployments."
