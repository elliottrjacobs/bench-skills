# Landing Page Anatomy & Structure

The proven section order for high-converting landing pages. Every section has a specific job — skip or reorder only with good reason.

## Section-by-Section Breakdown

### 1. Hero Section

**Job:** Convey the value proposition in under 5 seconds.

**Components:**
- **Headline** — Benefit-driven, specific, under 10 words. 8/10 people read the headline; only 2/10 read further. This is the most important piece of copy on the page.
- **Subheadline** — Clarifies *how* the benefit is delivered. 1-2 sentences max.
- **Primary CTA** — Action-oriented button. Never "Submit" or "Click Here." Use outcome language: "Start free trial", "Get the guide", "See it in action."
- **Hero visual** — Product screenshot in context, demo GIF, or illustration that reinforces the headline. Must support the message, not decorate.

**Rules:**
- Everything above the fold: headline, subheadline, CTA, and visual
- No navigation links (remove the header nav — it's an escape route)
- Message must match the ad/email/link that brought the visitor here

**Test:** Show the hero to someone for 5 seconds. Can they tell you what the product does and who it's for? If not, rewrite.

---

### 2. Social Proof Bar

**Job:** Build immediate credibility before asking the visitor to read further.

**Options (pick 1-2):**
- Client/partner logos (5-7 recognizable brands)
- User count or metric ("Trusted by 10,000+ teams")
- One standout testimonial quote
- Press mentions or "As seen in" logos
- Star rating with review count

**Placement:** Directly below the hero, before any body copy. Trust must be established early.

---

### 3. Pain / Problem Section

**Job:** Show the visitor you understand their frustration. Make them feel seen.

**Approach:**
- Name 2-3 specific pain points the target audience experiences
- Use the visitor's own language (pull from reviews, support tickets, interviews)
- Agitate — make the cost of inaction clear
- Do NOT pitch the solution yet. Sit with the problem first.

**Framework:** Use PAS (Problem → Agitate → Solution) or the Pain step of PRESTO. See [copywriting-frameworks.md](copywriting-frameworks.md).

---

### 4. Benefits Section

**Job:** Show the outcomes the customer gets (not what the product does).

**Structure:** 3-4 blocks, each with:
- Short heading (outcome-focused)
- 1-2 sentence description
- Optional: icon or small visual

**Rules:**
- Benefits, not features. "Ship 2x faster" not "CI/CD pipeline integration."
- Each benefit should map to a pain point from Section 3
- Be specific: "Save 10 hours/week" beats "Save time"

---

### 5. How It Works

**Job:** Reduce perceived complexity. Show the path from "now" to "outcome."

**Structure:** 3 steps (never more than 4):
1. Number + short heading + one sentence
2. Number + short heading + one sentence
3. Number + short heading + one sentence

**Rules:**
- Start from the visitor's current state, end at the promised outcome
- Use simple, everyday language
- If the process is genuinely complex, abstract it — the goal is confidence, not a technical walkthrough

---

### 6. Social Proof / Case Study

**Job:** Provide deep proof that the promised outcomes are real.

**Options:**
- 2-3 testimonials with real names, roles, company, and specific results
- Mini case study: before/after with metrics
- Video testimonial (30-60 seconds)
- Data visualization showing aggregate results

**Rules:**
- Specific results always beat vague praise: "Cut deploy time from 45 min to 3 min" > "Great product!"
- Include role/title to help visitors see themselves in the testimonial
- Real photos outperform stock images
- Place the strongest testimonial closest to the CTA

---

### 7. Feature Deep-Dive (Optional)

**Job:** Satisfy solution-aware visitors who are comparing options.

**When to include:** Only when the audience is actively comparing solutions and needs to see specific capabilities.

**Structure:** Scannable grid or list:
- Icon + heading + one line per feature
- Group by category if more than 6 features
- Link features back to benefits where possible

**When to skip:** If the audience is problem-aware but not yet solution-aware, features will confuse more than convince. Lead with benefits instead.

---

### 8. Trust & Risk Reversal

**Job:** Eliminate the last reasons NOT to convert.

**Elements:**
- Money-back guarantee with specific terms ("30-day no-questions-asked refund")
- Free trial language ("No credit card required")
- Security badges (SSL, SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA)
- Partner/certification logos
- Privacy commitment

**Rules:**
- Place near the CTA — reduce friction at the moment of decision
- Be specific about guarantees. Vague "satisfaction guaranteed" means nothing.

---

### 9. FAQ Section

**Job:** Address the top objections that prevent conversion.

**Structure:** 3-5 questions, each with a direct, honest answer.

**How to choose questions:**
- Map to the objections identified in strategy (Step 3 of Create Mode)
- Common themes: pricing, implementation time, switching costs, security, support
- Pull from actual customer questions (sales calls, support tickets, chat logs)

**Rules:**
- Answer directly. Don't dodge or deflect.
- Keep answers to 2-3 sentences. If it needs more, link to a docs page.
- Use structured data markup (FAQ schema) for SEO/AEO benefits.

---

### 10. Final CTA

**Job:** Give one last push for visitors who scrolled the entire page.

**Structure:**
- Restate the value proposition from a different angle than the hero
- Address the emotional state of someone who read everything but hasn't committed
- Repeat the exact same CTA button as the hero

**Rules:**
- Don't introduce new information here
- Can add light urgency if genuine ("Limited beta spots", "Price increases March 1")
- Never use fake urgency or dark patterns

## Design Principles

| Principle | Why |
|-----------|-----|
| **One CTA, repeated 2-3 times** | Single focused goal. Repeat at hero, mid-page, and final section. |
| **Remove all navigation** | No header nav, no footer links. Every escape route costs conversions. |
| **Visual hierarchy → CTA** | The eye should flow: headline → visual → CTA button. Nothing competes. |
| **High-contrast CTA button** | The button must be the most visually prominent element on the page. |
| **Whitespace over clutter** | Every element needs breathing room. Clutter kills comprehension. |
| **Mobile-first** | 62%+ of traffic is mobile. Design for thumbs, not cursors. |
| **Load time under 3 seconds** | 53% of mobile users abandon after 3s. Compress images, minimize scripts. |
| **Minimal form fields** | Every extra field drops conversion. Ask only for what you absolutely need. |

## Conversion Benchmarks

- Pages with a single CTA: up to 62% higher conversion than multi-CTA pages
- Reducing page complexity: up to 27% improvement in conversion rates
- Video on landing pages: up to 86% lift in conversions
- Live chat on landing pages: ~20% increase in sales
- Testimonials appear on 36% of top-performing landing pages
