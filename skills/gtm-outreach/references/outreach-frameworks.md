# Outreach Frameworks

Distilled methodology. Sources: Pete Kazanjy, Jen Abel, Jeanne DeWitt Grosser, Matt Dixon.

---

## Sequence Architecture

### Cold Outbound (12-18 touches, 3-4 weeks)

**Principles:**
- Multi-channel > single-channel (email + LinkedIn + phone = 3x response rate vs email alone)
- First touch must earn the second. No "just checking in."
- Every touch adds value or a new angle — never repeat the same message
- Personalization tier determines effort per account

**Cadence Template:**
| Day | Channel | Purpose | Notes |
|-----|---------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Email | The hook — specific pain, trigger, or insight | Under 80 words |
| 2 | LinkedIn | Connect + short note referencing email | Do NOT pitch in connect |
| 4 | Email | Value add — share insight, data, resource | Position as helpful, not salesy |
| 7 | LinkedIn | Engage with their content (like, comment) | Build familiarity |
| 9 | Email | Proof — case study, specific result | "[Similar company] saw [result]" |
| 11 | Phone | Direct call + voicemail | Reference emails, keep to 30 sec |
| 14 | Email | New angle — different hook or pain point | Try a different persona pain |
| 18 | LinkedIn | Direct message — reference all prior touches | "I've tried reaching you..." |
| 21 | Email | Breakup — leave door open | Acknowledge, restate value, final CTA |

### Warm Follow-Up (6-8 touches, 2 weeks)

For inbound leads, event contacts, or referrals:
| Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 0 | Email | Acknowledge the trigger (download, event, referral) |
| 1 | LinkedIn | Connect with context |
| 3 | Email | Value add specific to their interest |
| 5 | Phone | Quick call to connect |
| 7 | Email | Case study relevant to their situation |
| 10 | Email | Direct ask with specific next step |

### Re-Engagement (4-5 touches, 2 weeks)

For cold leads or lost deals:
| Day | Channel | Purpose |
|-----|---------|---------|
| 1 | Email | "Things have changed" — new feature, case study, or angle |
| 4 | LinkedIn | Share relevant content |
| 7 | Email | New proof point or different hook |
| 10 | Email | Direct ask: "Is this still a priority?" |
| 14 | Email | Final: "Should I close this out?" |

## Email Copywriting for Outreach

### Subject Lines That Work
- Specific > clever: "[Their company] + [your value]"
- Question that implies knowledge: "How is [company] handling [specific challenge]?"
- Referencing a trigger: "Congrats on [funding/hire/launch]"
- Avoid: "Quick question", "Following up", "Introduction", all caps, emojis

### First Line Rules
- Reference something specific about THEM (not you)
- Bad: "I help companies like yours..."
- Good: "Noticed you just hired 3 data engineers — usually means [pain point]"
- The first line earns the second line. That's its only job.

### Body Rules
- One pain point per email (not three)
- Under 80 words for cold email, under 120 for warm
- Write at a 5th grade reading level
- No jargon. No marketing-speak. Write like a human.
- Social proof in one line: "[Company] saw [result]"

### CTA Rules
- One ask per email (never two)
- Low commitment: "Would a 15-minute call make sense?"
- Interest-based: "Worth exploring, or off base?"
- Resource offer: "Want me to send the [relevant resource]?"
- Avoid: "Let me know your availability", "When can we chat?"

## Founder-Led Sales (Kazanjy / Abel)

### Why Founders Must Sell First
- No one knows the product and vision like the founder
- Early conversations refine the product
- Can't hire salespeople until you've closed 10-20 deals yourself
- The process you develop becomes the sales playbook

### Founder Outreach Principles
- Authenticity > polish. Be a founder, not a salesperson.
- Lead with curiosity, not a pitch: "I'm building X and researching how [their role] handles Y"
- Use warm intros aggressively (investors, advisors, first customers)
- Volume matters: aim for 5-10 new conversations per week
- Track everything: what resonates, what falls flat, exact words that work

## Signal-Based Prospecting

### High-Intent Signals
| Signal | Source | Why It Matters |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Hiring for relevant role | LinkedIn, job boards | Indicates budget and priority |
| Recent funding | Crunchbase, news | Budget available, growth mandate |
| Tech stack change | BuiltWith, job postings | Indicates infrastructure investment |
| Competitor mentions | G2, social, forums | Actively evaluating solutions |
| Content engagement | Website analytics, LinkedIn | Showing interest in problem space |
| Expansion signals | Office openings, new markets | Growth creating new needs |

### Trigger-Based Sequences
When a trigger fires, immediately enter the prospect into a relevant sequence:
- Funding trigger → "Congrats on the raise" sequence
- Hiring trigger → "Scaling your team" sequence
- Competitor review → "Exploring alternatives" sequence
- Content download → "Based on your interest in X" sequence
