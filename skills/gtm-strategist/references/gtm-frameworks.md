# GTM Frameworks

Distilled go-to-market methodology. Sources: Jeanne DeWitt Grosser, Emily Kramer, Geoffrey Moore, Elena Verna, Seth Godin.

---

## Jeanne DeWitt Grosser — World-Class GTM

Source: Lenny's Podcast ("What world-class GTM looks like in 2026"), Stripe/Google/Vercel COO.

### GTM as Product
Treat your go-to-market as a product — it should be engineered, measured, and iterated on with the same rigor as your software product.

### The GTM Engineer Role
A new hybrid role emerging at top companies — sits at the intersection of sales, marketing, and product. Responsibilities:
- Builds and optimizes the GTM motion as a system
- Uses data to identify the best segments, channels, and motions
- Automates outreach and qualification with AI agents
- Measures pipeline efficiency end-to-end

### Segmentation Framework
Segment your market on 3 dimensions:
- **Size** — Company size / deal size
- **Growth rate** — Are they expanding or stable?
- **Workload fit** — How well does your product fit their workflow?

The intersection of high growth + high workload fit = your best segment, regardless of size.

### Discovery as Sales Skill
- "80% of buyers buy to avoid pain, not to gain benefit"
- The best sellers are great at discovery — understanding the buyer's situation before pitching
- Build vs. buy calculus for AI agents: companies underestimate the cost of building internally

### AI in GTM
- AI agents are replacing SDR busywork (10 SDRs → 1 SDR + agents)
- Gong's deal-bots analyze every sales call and flag risk
- The future GTM team is smaller, more skilled, more leveraged by AI
- "Company universe" approach: map every potential customer, score them, then target precisely

## Emily Kramer — Fuel vs. Engine

Source: Lenny's Podcast, MKT1 newsletter.

### The Core Framework
Marketing has two halves:
- **Fuel** = content, copy, positioning, messaging, creative — what you say and how you say it
- **Engine** = distribution, channels, operations, automation — how you get it in front of people

Most marketing failures happen because companies invest in fuel without building the engine, or vice versa.

### Pi-Shaped Marketers
The best marketing hires are "pi-shaped":
- Expert in 1 area (deep expertise)
- Proficient in a 2nd area (can do it well)
- Breadth across all marketing functions (understands the whole)

### GACCS Framework for Campaigns
1. **Goals** — What metric are you trying to move?
2. **Audience** — Who specifically are you targeting?
3. **Creative** — What's the message, format, and hook?
4. **Channels** — Where will you distribute this?
5. **Stakeholders** — Who needs to be involved and what's the DRI?

### Key Principles
- Product marketing is the "fuel-heavy generalist" — they own positioning, messaging, and launch strategy
- DRI lists prevent cross-functional chaos — one person owns each decision
- Marketing should be measured on pipeline contribution, not vanity metrics
- "The best marketing feels like the product talking, not the marketing team talking"

## Geoffrey Moore — Beachhead GTM

Source: *Crossing the Chasm*, Lenny's Podcast.

### The Whole Product Concept
Your core product is rarely enough. For GTM, you need the "whole product":
- Core product (what you build)
- Extended product (integrations, APIs, partnerships)
- Expected product (support, documentation, onboarding)
- Augmented product (training, community, professional services)

The chasm exists because early adopters will assemble the whole product themselves, but the mainstream won't.

### GTM Sequencing
1. **Beachhead** — Dominate one narrow segment first
2. **Bowling pin** — Use beachhead references to knock down adjacent segments
3. **Tornado** — When demand exceeds supply, ride the wave
4. **Main street** — Mature market, optimize for retention and expansion

## Elena Verna — Growth Principles

Source: Lenny's Podcast (2 episodes), Reforge.

### 10 Growth Tactics That Fail
1. Paid acquisition as primary growth engine (it's rented, not owned)
2. Referral programs without organic word of mouth
3. Growth teams without product-market fit
4. Virality features without inherent value
5. Content marketing without distribution strategy
6. A/B testing without hypotheses
7. Copying competitor tactics without understanding their context
8. Hiring "growth hackers" instead of building growth culture
9. Optimizing conversion without fixing retention
10. Ignoring earned channels (SEO, community, word of mouth)

### Growth for AI Companies
- PMF must be recaptured every 3 months as models improve
- Traditional growth playbooks fail for AI because the product changes so fast
- Focus on workflow integration, not standalone features
- Habit formation is harder when capabilities shift constantly

### Earned vs. Paid Channels
- Earned channels (SEO, community, word of mouth) compound over time
- Paid channels decay — you stop paying, growth stops
- The best companies build earned channel foundations early, then layer paid on top

## Seth Godin — Minimum Viable Audience

Source: *This is Marketing*, Lenny's Podcast.

### Core Principles
- **Don't find customers for your product. Find products for your customers.**
- Start with the smallest viable audience — "Who's it for?" is the most important question
- Remarkable = worth remarking on. If people don't talk about it, it's not remarkable.
- Permission marketing > interruption marketing. Earn attention, don't buy it.
- "People like us do things like this" — marketing is about identity and belonging

### Distribution Choices
- Choose channels where your audience already gathers
- Build trust before asking for the sale
- Community-first > advertising-first for most products

## Growth Case Studies (Distilled Patterns)

### Bottom-Up / PLG (Figma, Notion, Calendly)
- Free tier gets individual users hooked
- Users bring the product into their team
- Team adoption triggers enterprise sales conversation
- Key metric: organic user-to-team expansion rate

### Community-Led (Notion, Duolingo, Figma)
- Invest in community before you invest in sales
- User-generated content creates SEO moat
- Champions within companies become your sales force
- Camille Ricketts (Notion): community-led growth to $10B

### Content/SEO (Deel, HubSpot)
- Meltem Kuran (Deel): $0 to $300M ARR via Reddit/SEO/low-cost channels
- Create content that answers the questions your buyers are asking
- Distribution > creation — 80% of effort should be getting content seen

### Micro-Influencer (Gamma)
- Grant Lee (Gamma): $100M ARR via micro-influencer strategy
- Thousands of small creators > a few big influencers
- Authentic demonstrations > sponsored posts

### Word of Mouth (Wise, Superhuman)
- Nilan Peiris (Wise): word-of-mouth as primary growth driver
- Product quality → word of mouth → organic growth
- "Remarkable quality means people can't help but tell others" (Rahul Vohra)
