# Content & Copywriting Frameworks

Distilled methodology. Sources: Eugene Schwartz, David Ogilvy, Donald Miller, Ann Handley, Joe Pulizzi, Robert Cialdini, Russell Brunson.

---

## Schwartz — 5 Levels of Awareness

| Level | State | Your Job | Headline Approach |
|-------|-------|----------|------------------|
| Most Aware | Knows product, needs the deal | Make the offer | Direct: price, bonus, urgency |
| Product Aware | Knows you, not convinced | Prove claims | Benefit + proof: "Here's why..." |
| Solution Aware | Knows solutions, not yours | Position your solution | Lead with mechanism: "The [method] that..." |
| Problem Aware | Feels pain, doesn't know solutions | Agitate then solve | Lead with problem: "Tired of...?" |
| Unaware | Doesn't know they have a problem | Educate through story | Lead with story/identity |

You cannot make someone aware. Meet them where they are and lead one level forward.

## Copy Structures

**PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)** — Best for ads, email hooks, social.
**AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)** — Best for landing pages, long-form.
**BAB (Before-After-Bridge)** — Best for case studies, transformation stories.

---

## Ogilvy — Headlines & Copy

- 5x more people read headlines than body. Headline must sell.
- Include benefit and specificity: "Reduces costs by 37%" beats "Saves money"
- Long copy sells when reader is interested. Don't fear length — fear boring.
- Specifics persuade: "731 companies" beats "hundreds of companies"

## Miller — StoryBrand

Customer is the hero. Brand is the guide. 7 elements:
1. Character (customer) wants something
2. Has a problem (external, internal, philosophical)
3. Meets a guide (you — show empathy + authority)
4. Who gives a plan (3 steps — make it simple)
5. Calls to action (direct + transitional CTA)
6. Helps avoid failure (stakes of inaction)
7. Ends in success (the transformation)

## Handley — Content Marketing Rules

- Utility × Inspiration × Empathy = great content
- "So what?" test after every sentence
- Write for one person, not an audience
- Show, don't tell — replace adjectives with specifics

---

## Pulizzi — Content Strategy

### Content Tilt
Don't just cover a topic. Find the angle no one else owns.
"Content marketing for SaaS" = topic. "Content marketing for bootstrapped B2B SaaS under $10M" = tilt.

### One Channel First
Dominate one platform with one format before expanding.

## Cialdini — 6 Persuasion Principles

| Principle | In Content |
|-----------|-----------|
| Reciprocity | Give value first (free resources, insights) |
| Commitment | Start with small asks ("Do you agree...?") |
| Social Proof | Testimonials, user counts, logos |
| Authority | Expert endorsements, credentials, data |
| Liking | Relatability, shared values, personality |
| Scarcity | Limited availability, deadlines (use honestly) |

## SEO/AEO Strategy

### Search Engine Optimization
- Target topics, not just keywords — build topic clusters
- Answer the question in the first 100 words (featured snippet optimization)
- Internal linking between related content pieces
- Update evergreen content quarterly

### Answer Engine Optimization (AEO)
- Optimize for AI citation: clear answers, structured data, authoritative sourcing
- Use headers that match natural language questions
- Include data and specific claims AI can cite
- Build topical authority through depth, not breadth

## Case Study Framework

### Collection Process
1. Identify candidates: NPS promoters, high-usage accounts, expansion customers
2. Request interview: 30 min, focus on their story (not your product)
3. Interview structure: Situation → Challenge → Solution → Results → Quote
4. Draft and get approval (let them edit the quote, not the story)
5. Publish in multiple formats: full case study, quote card, social snippet, email insert

### Before-After-Bridge Template
- **Before:** What was life like? (Specific metrics, pain, frustration)
- **After:** What changed? (Specific metrics, outcomes, feelings)
- **Bridge:** How did they get there? (Your product/service as the vehicle)

### Platform-Specific Conventions

| Platform | Key Rules |
|----------|----------|
| LinkedIn | Hook in first 2 lines, short paragraphs, personal voice, 3-5 hashtags |
| Twitter/X | 280 chars, front-load value, threads for depth, no hashtags in body |
| Email | 6-10 word subject, one CTA, mobile-first, P.S. gets read |
| Blog | H2/H3 structure, answer question in intro, internal links, 1500-2500 words |
